Biological Sex

The Mifflin-St Jeor equation requires your biological sex (assigned at birth) to determine baseline caloric needs accurately.

Men typically have a higher percentage of lean body mass, while women naturally carry a higher level of essential fat tissue to support endocrine and reproductive health. Biological differences modify the base BMR formula coefficients significantly.

Activity Levels

Sedentary: 🛋️🪑

Mostly seated work. Little to no planned movement outside of daily life.

Lightly Active: 🚶‍♀️🧘

You stay on your feet or get in 1–3 light movement sessions (walking, yoga, light chores) per week.

Moderately Active: 🏃‍♀️💪

Consistent training 3–5 times per week. You break a sweat and prioritize structured sessions.

Highly Active: 🏋️‍♀️🤸‍♀️

Training is a habit. You’re moving or in the gym 6–7 days a week with a mix of strength and conditioning.

Extremely Active: 🥇🚀

Performance is your craft. Daily physical labor or high-volume, multi-session training is your norm.

Circular Roots Nutrition Protocol

Protein Strategy (1g per lb)

Consuming 1g of protein per pound of bodyweight preserves muscle tissue while in a deficit and fuels lean mass development during a surplus. This is a foundational, non-negotiable target. (Macro values are rounded to the nearest 5g).

Fat Strategy (25% Target)

Fixed at 25% of total target calories to protect optimal hormone health, joint function, and cognitive performance.

Carbohydrate Strategy (Remainder)

Carbs occupy the remainder of your calories to maximize athletic fuel. Carbs are essential for powering explosive barbell work, kettlebell complexes, and high-intensity movement sessions.

Top Coach Tip

"Calculators are starting points, not laws. Track your body weight for 2 to 4 weeks alongside your training. Adjust calories up or down by 100-200 if you notice stalled progression or energy depletion. Consistency is the true accelerator."
